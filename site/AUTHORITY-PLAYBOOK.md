## What matters most

1. Google Search Console verification and sitemap submission
2. Bing Webmaster verification and sitemap submission
3. Google Business Profile optimization for Dubai
4. Consistent business citations across reputable directories
5. Real backlinks from relevant businesses, publications, partners, and industry profiles
6. Ongoing publication of useful first-party content tied to real services and proof

## Quality backlink sources to pursue

Prioritize relevance and trust over raw count.

- Google Business Profile website link
- Bing Places profile
- Clutch
- GoodFirms
- Crunchbase, if appropriate for the business profile
- Behance and Dribbble profile links where the work is genuinely showcased
- LinkedIn company or professional profile
- Industry associations, chambers, and local Dubai business communities
- Partner or supplier websites with legitimate partner pages
- Local UAE business directories with editorial standards
- Guest contributions on relevant marketing, SEO, web, or Dubai business publications
- Podcast, interview, and founder-feature pages with a canonical link back to the site

## Links to avoid

- Paid link farms
- Private blog networks
- Low-quality general directories
- Bulk profile creation on irrelevant sites
- Comment spam
- Forum signature spam
- Exact-match anchor campaigns at scale

## Outreach angles that are more likely to earn links

- Publish a practical Dubai SEO checklist and pitch it as a useful reference
- Publish a lead-generation systems guide with real implementation detail
- Turn case studies into partner co-marketing assets
- Offer expert commentary for local business or marketing publications
- Create a simple media kit or professional bio page for interviews and features

## Measurement

- Referring domains
- Links from locally relevant UAE sites
- Branded search growth
- Search Console impressions and clicks for service pages
- Ranking movement on Dubai commercial queries
- Qualified enquiries from organic search

## Rule

Do not chase link count. Chase relevant referring domains that would make sense even if search engines did not exist.
